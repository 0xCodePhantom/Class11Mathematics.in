Chapter 14: Probability – Important Questions

Q1. A card is drawn from a well-shuffled deck of 52 cards. Find the probability of getting (i) a red card, (ii) a king.

Total cards = 52. Red cards = 26. Kings = 4.
(i) P(Red) = 26/52 = 1/2
(ii) P(King) = 4/52 = 1/13

Q2. Two dice are thrown together. Find the probability of getting a sum of 7.

Possible sums of 7: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1) → 6 outcomes.
Total outcomes = 36.
P(Sum=7) = 6/36 = 1/6

Q3. A coin is tossed 3 times. Find the probability of getting exactly 2 heads.

Sample space size = 2³ = 8.
Favourable: HHT, HTH, THH (3 outcomes).
Probability = 3/8.

Q4. A die is thrown. Find the probability of getting a number greater than 4.

Numbers greater than 4: 5,6 (2 outcomes).
Total = 6.
Probability = 2/6 = 1/3.

Q5. A bag has 3 red and 5 black balls. One ball is drawn. Find the probability it is red.

Total balls = 8, Red = 3.
Probability = 3/8.

Q6. Find the probability of getting at least one head when 2 coins are tossed.

Sample space = {HH, HT, TH, TT}.
At least one head: HH, HT, TH (3 outcomes).
Probability = 3/4.

Q7. A box contains 5 white and 7 black balls. One ball is drawn at random. Find the probability that it is black.

Total = 12, Black = 7.
Probability = 7/12.

Q8. Two cards are drawn from a deck. Find the probability that both are aces.

P(1st Ace) = 4/52.
P(2nd Ace) = 3/51.
Probability = (4/52) × (3/51) = 12/2652 = 1/221.

Q9. A die is thrown twice. Find the probability of getting an even number on both throws.

Even outcomes = {2,4,6} → 3/6 = 1/2.
Both throws: (1/2) × (1/2) = 1/4.

Q10. A family has 2 children. Find the probability that both are boys if at least one is a boy.

Sample space = {BB, BG, GB, GG}.
Given at least one boy → {BB, BG, GB}.
Probability(both boys) = 1/3.
