Chapter 3: Trigonometric Functions

Important Questions with Step-by-Step Answers (Class 11 CBSE 2025–26)

Q1. Find the radian measure corresponding to 60°.
To convert degrees to radians, multiply by π/180.
60° × (π/180) = π/3 radians.
Q2. Find the degree measure corresponding to 5π/6 radians.
Multiply radians by 180/π.
(5π/6) × (180/π) = 150°.
Q3. Draw the graph of y = sin x for x ∈ [0, 2π].
The sine function starts at 0, rises to 1 at π/2, returns to 0 at π, dips to -1 at 3π/2, and returns to 0 at 2π.
Q4. Verify sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.
From the Pythagoras theorem in the unit circle, for any point (cos θ, sin θ):
cos²θ + sin²θ = 1² = 1.
Hence verified.
Q5. Find the general solution of sin x = √3/2.
sin x = √3/2 at x = π/3 and 2π/3.
General solution: x = nπ + (-1)n π/3, n ∈ ℤ.
Q6. Solve cos x = -1/2.
cos x = -1/2 at x = 2π/3 and 4π/3.
General solution: x = 2nπ ± 2π/3, n ∈ ℤ.
Q7. Express tan θ in terms of sin θ.
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ.
Using cos²θ = 1 - sin²θ → cos θ = √(1 - sin²θ).
Hence tan θ = sin θ / √(1 - sin²θ).
Q8. Find the principal value of sin-1(-1/2).
The principal value range of sin-1 is [−π/2, π/2].
sin(−π/6) = −1/2. So, answer = −π/6.
Q9. Find the principal value of tan-1(√3).
The principal value range of tan-1 is (−π/2, π/2).
tan(π/3) = √3. So, answer = π/3.
Q10. Show that cos(π − θ) = −cos θ.
cos(π − θ) = cos π cos θ + sin π sin θ.
= (−1)(cos θ) + (0)(sin θ) = −cos θ.
Hence proved.
