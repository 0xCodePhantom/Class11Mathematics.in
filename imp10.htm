Chapter 10: Conic Sections

Important questions, clear explanations and inline SVG diagrams (Class 11 CBSE).
Overview
Conic sections are the curves obtained by intersecting a plane with a double-napped cone: circle, parabola, ellipse and hyperbola. Each has a standard equation and characteristic parameters (radius/vertex/foci/eccentricity).
Q1. Write the standard equation of a circle with centre (h,k) and radius r. How to find equation when centre is (2,−1) and r=3?
Standard form: (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r². For centre (2,−1) and r=3: (x−2)² + (y+1)² = 9.
Q2. Define parabola and write its standard equation with vertex at origin. Give equation for parabola with focus (0,2) and directrix y=−2.
Parabola: set of points equidistant from a fixed point (focus) and a fixed line (directrix). Standard (vertex at origin): y² = 4ax or x² = 4ay depending on axis. If focus is (0,2) and directrix y=−2, distance from vertex to focus a=2, equation y = (1/(4a)) x²? More directly, parabola with vertical axis: x² = 4ay where a=2 ⇒ x² = 8y. (Equivalently write y = x²/8.)
Q3. Define ellipse and write its standard equation (major axis along x-axis). What is eccentricity?
Ellipse (major axis along x): x²/a² + y²/b² = 1 with a>b>0. Foci at (±c,0) where c² = a² − b². Eccentricity e = c/a where 0<e<1; it measures how elongated the ellipse is.
Q4. Define hyperbola and give standard equation x²/a² − y²/b² = 1. How to find asymptotes?
Hyperbola with transverse axis along x: x²/a² − y²/b² = 1. Asymptotes: y = ±(b/a)x for centre at origin. They guide the branches' directions.
Q5. If an ellipse has a=5 and b=4, find c and eccentricity e.
c² = a² − b² = 25 − 16 = 9 ⇒ c = 3. e = c/a = 3/5 = 0.6.
Q6. Show that the parabola y² = 4ax has focus (a,0) and directrix x=−a.
Distance from (x,y) to focus (a,0) equals distance to line x=−a: √[(x−a)² + y²] = x + a. Squaring and simplifying yields y² = 4ax. Thus focus and directrix are as stated.
Q7. Find equation of tangent to circle x² + y² = 25 at point (3,4).
For circle x² + y² = r², tangent at (x₁,y₁) is xx₁ + yy₁ = r². Here: 3x + 4y = 25.
Q8. For hyperbola x²/9 − y²/16 = 1, find asymptotes and centre.
Centre at origin (0,0). a²=9 ⇒ a=3, b²=16 ⇒ b=4. Asymptotes: y = ±(b/a)x = ±(4/3)x.
Q9. If a conic has eccentricity e = 1, identify the curve and explain.
e = 1 corresponds to a parabola (boundary between ellipse e<1 and hyperbola e>1). Parabola has single focus line directrix property with e=1.
Q10. Word problem: A reflector uses a parabola y² = 12x. Where should the receiver be placed (distance from vertex)?
For y² = 4ax, compare 4a = 12 ⇒ a = 3. Receiver (focus) at (a,0) from vertex: at (3,0) — 3 units from vertex along axis.
