Chapter 6: Permutations & Combinations

Clear, step-by-step solutions and helpful diagrams (Class 11 CBSE).
Q1
What is 5! and why? Explain factorial meaning.
5! = 5×4×3×2×1 = 120. Factorial n! counts the number of ways to arrange n distinct objects in a line. For 5 objects, first position has 5 choices, second 4, and so on; multiply gives 120.
Q2
How many ways to arrange 7 different books on a shelf?
Arrangements = 7! = 5040. Reason: each arrangement is a permutation of 7 distinct items.
Q3
How many 4-letter codewords can be made from letters A,B,C,D (repetition allowed)?
Repetition allowed, order matters. For each of 4 positions there are 4 choices ⇒ 4⁴ = 256 codewords. Visualize as a 4-level tree: each level has 4 branches.
Partial tree: illustrates branching. Full tree would have 4⁴ leaves.
Q4
Find number of permutations of 5 objects taken 3 at a time (nP r).
nPr = n!/(n−r)!. So 5P3 = 5×4×3 = 60. Explanation: choose ordered list of length 3 from 5 distinct objects: choices: 5 for 1st, 4 for 2nd, 3 for 3rd.
Q5
How many ways to select a committee of 3 from 10 students (order doesn't matter)?
Use combinations nCr = n!/(r!(n−r)!). So C(10,3) = 10×9×8/(3×2×1) = 120. Explanation: choose 3 people; order doesn't matter.
Q6
How many distinct words can be formed from letters of the word "LEVEL"?
Letters: L (2), E (2), V (1). Total arrangements = 5!/(2!2!1!) = 120/(2×2)=30. Reason: divide by factorials of identical-letter counts to remove overcounting.
Q7
In how many ways can 6 people be seated around a round table?
Circular permutations of n distinct items = (n−1)!. So (6−1)! = 5! = 120 ways. Reason: rotations are considered identical, so fix one seat and permute the rest.
Circular seating: rotate to match arrangements — so divide linear permutations by n.
Q8
From digits 0–9, how many 3-digit numbers can be formed with no repetition and leading digit non-zero?
Choices for 1st digit: 1–9 → 9 choices. For 2nd: any remaining digit (including 0) → 9 choices. For 3rd: remaining 8 choices. Total = 9×9×8 = 648.
Q9
In how many ways can the letters of the word "BANANA" be arranged?
BANANA has 6 letters with counts: B:1, A:3, N:2. Arrangements = 6!/(3!2!1!) = 720/(6×2)=60.
Q10
Using complementary counting: How many ways to choose 3 students from 8 so that at least one is a girl? (There are 3 girls out of 8.)
Total ways to choose 3 from 8: C(8,3)=56. Ways with no girls (all boys): choose 3 from 5 boys: C(5,3)=10. So at least one girl = 56−10 = 46.
